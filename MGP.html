Models of good practice


Logo in the top left
100% of the websites researched had a clickable logo in the upper left corner of every page on the site.By this point in time it a standart I cant think of a website that had its logo somewhere else

Contact in top right
44% have the contact button or link in the top right corner of every page. Although this placement is very common and considered best practices, it cannot be considered standard. Me and my course mates with we talked about our design decisions place our contact form in top right

Main navigation across the top
88% of the websites had the main navigation located in the header at the top of every page, making horizontal top-level navigation a web design standard. That is quite debatable because some of my mates from my course used vertical navigation

Responsive design
68% of websites are mobile-friendly using responsive web design. This gives visitors a great experience regardless of the device – phone, tablet or desktop.We were required to make mobile first webpage on the assignment

Degrade gracefully
if a whizzy new feature is decided upon for users with the most modern browsers, an alternative should be provided for people with older browsers that can't deal with the technology.For example alt tags on images or videos so that user whose browser cant load that content for one or other reason can have an idea what that content is about.

"Anyone who slaps a 'this page is best viewed with Browser X' label on a web page appears to be yearning for the bad old days, before the web, when you had very little chance of reading a document written on another computer, another word processor, or another network." Tim Berners-Lee, 1996.
